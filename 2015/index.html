PyCon schedule on Guidebook!

by Brian Curtin

We have once again partnered with Guidebook to provide an excellent web and mobile schedule for you to keep track of everything PyCon.

To download Guidebook, visit the Apple App Store, the Android Market Place, or https://guidebook.com/getit/ and then enter the code: pycon15.

Fourth annual 5K Fun Run benefitting Autism Speaks

by Brian Curtin

We're really happy to be holding our fourth annual 5K Charity Fun Run on Saturday April 11 at 7 AM, before day two of PyCon kicks off. The event was introduced in Santa Clara and has been fun for everyone involved and each year has raised money for a bunch of great causes. This year's proceeds benefit Autism Speaks Canada!

Explore Montreal

by Diana Clarke

PyCon 2015 is in the center of an exciting city full of great food, culture, and history. It would be a shame to not check it out while you're in town!

In addition to the conference's five tracks of talks, there will be a sixth track an opportunity for you to explore Montral with free guided tours.

About

PyCon is the largest annual gathering for the community using and developing the open-source Python programming language. [PyCon is organized by the Python community for the community](/2015/about/what-is-pycon/ ""). We try to keep registration far cheaper than most comparable technology conferences, to keep PyCon accessible to the widest group possible. PyCon is a [diverse conference](/2015/about/diversity/ "") dedicated to providing an enjoyable experience to everyone. Our [code of conduct](/2015/about/code-of-conduct/ "") is intended to help everyone maintain the PyCon spirit. We thank all attendees and staff for observing it.

PyCon 2015 in Montréal is a production of the Python Software Foundation.
